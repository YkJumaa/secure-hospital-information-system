# Secure-Hospital-Information-System

The Secure Hospital Information System is a digital platform designed to bring order, accountability, and speed to patient data management within healthcare facilities. Traditional paper‑based or fragmented digital methods such as handwritten notes, isolated spreadsheets, or separate department logs, often lead to delays, lost information, and lack of role‑appropriate access. This system eliminates those gaps by providing a structured, transparent, and role‑based way to manage patient records, appointments, vitals, and staff activities.

🎯 The Problem

Hospitals and clinics face recurring challenges:

Delayed clinical decisions: Patient histories are scattered, causing slow diagnosis and treatment.
Poor record‑keeping: Without a centralised system, medical records get lost or are incomplete.
Lack of access control: Sensitive health data may be viewed or edited by unauthorised personnel.
Fragmented communication: Doctors, nurses, receptionists, and administrators operate in silos.
No audit trail: It is difficult to track who accessed or changed patient information.
These issues compromise patient safety, data privacy, and operational efficiency.

💡 The Solution

The Secure Hospital Information System solves these challenges by:

Centralising records: All patient data, medical records, prescriptions, and vitals are stored in one unified database.
Enforcing role‑based access (RBAC): Admin, Doctor, Nurse, Receptionist, and Patient each have tailored permissions – no one sees what they shouldn’t.
Streamlining workflows: Doctors update diagnoses and prescriptions; nurses record vitals; receptionists register patients and schedule appointments.
Ensuring accountability: Every action (login, record update, appointment change) is logged with timestamp, user, role, and IP address.
Improving patient safety: Allergies and chronic conditions are clearly displayed to clinicians.
Automatic session timeout: Inactive users are logged out after 15 minutes, preventing unauthorised access.

🚀 Impact

By digitising hospital operations with RBAC and audit logging:

Response times are reduced – clinicians access complete patient history instantly.
Data integrity improves – no lost files or conflicting versions.
Patients feel safer – they can view their own records and appointments, knowing that access is tightly controlled.
Staff accountability increases – every action is traceable.
Hospitals comply with regulations – the system meets the Kenya Data Protection Act (2019) and follows international standards (HIPAA/GDPR).
